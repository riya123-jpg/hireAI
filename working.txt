Why This Matters
Without throw err — the catch block handles the error completely. The error never escapes the hook. Your component's try/catch never sees it — so it always thinks the apply succeeded.
With throw err — after saving to Redux, the error is re-thrown and travels up to whoever called handleApply. Your component's catch block then catches it properly.
